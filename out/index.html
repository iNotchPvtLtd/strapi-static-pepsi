Pepsi's appeal stems from a combination of its taste, branding, and the broader PepsiCo business model. It's a globally recognized brand with a history of innovation and adaptation to consumer preferences.

Taste and Innovation:

Pepsi's formula has evolved over time, but it's known for a distinct taste profile, often perceived as sweeter and more citrusy than Coca-Cola.

Branding and Marketing:

Pepsi has a strong global presence and a history of impactful advertising campaigns, often targeting younger demographics and emphasizing themes of rebellion and individuality.

Historical Context:

Pepsi was invented in 1893 by Caleb Bradham and was initially marketed as "Brad's Drink" and later renamed Pepsi-Cola in 1898.
